Canadian Tire Ad

This video was made as part of a Public Relations assignment that required us create a marketing plan for a newly launched item that we had thought of. Our choice was the WiFi Wilderness Tent, a product that would be sold through Canadian Tire. Our target markets were not only campers but also festival goers, both markets being people who would be able to take advantage of the WiFi connection.

All of the animations in the video are original and were made using Adobe Animate. They were all brought into Adobe Premiere Pro, where they were aligned with both the music, a royalty free song found online, as well as with the voice over.

All animations and voices used in this video are original. Any logos belong to their respective copyright.

SADD Commercial

This animation was created for SADD, a brand that I was given as part of an assignment in which I was tasked with creating a marketing plan for a cause. The purpose of the commercial was to create an ad that played on the emotional side of people by telling a story over just using facts.

The creative process began by storyboarding the events that would take place. Once that was done I found the locations that I would shoot at, as well as found royalty-free images to use for the bar scene at the beginning. I filmed using a Canon Rebel T5 all of the outdoor scenes. Once all the filming was complete I began animating all of the figures using Adobe Animate. In order to give the 2D models the depth that they have within the footage I used green models on top of the certain aspects of the video they would be behind, and removed the color in Adobe Premiere Pro, where the bulk of the editing took place.

After completing the animations and lining them up with the video I had reached picture lock, and it was time to move on to the audio part of the video. I gathered sound effects online as well as recorded several of my own, and began aligning them with the footage. I chose to use no music in order to keep all of the other sounds clear and raw, to help keep their impact. Once the audio was finished being added I was done.

All the footage used in this video were original. The photo used at the beginning in the bar scene was found from copyright-free sources on the internet, and the audio files were original recordings or open use.
